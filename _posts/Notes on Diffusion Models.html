Notes on Diffusion Models

The idea of Diffusion Models comes from non-equilibrium thermodynamics, generally speaking, we slowly add random Gaussian noise to the data step by step, then learn to reverse the procedure to construct new satisfactory data. Imagine that when the forward steps are very long, the data will completely be a noise; So this model contains the potential to generate a new image by denoising a Gaussian noise step by step. This potential becomes a fact after experiments.

Forward Process

Let's take it more specifically, we take x0 from the real data distribution q(x), that is, x0∼q(x). Now the Forward Process means we add small amount of Gaussian noise step by step, and finally we get a sequence x1,x2,...,xT after T steps. We introduce the variance schedule {βt}t=1T to control the stepsize, where βt∈(0,1) for t=1,2,...,T. And we get

q(xt|xt−1)=N(xt,1−βtxt−1,βtI),q(x1:T|x0)=∏t=1Tq(xt|xt−1),

where N(xt,μ,ϵ) means a Gaussian(μ,ϵ) with output being xt, and q(x1:T|x0) implies a joint distribution(a trajectory) of x1,...,xT given x0.

Reverse Process

The Reverse Process is to denoise from xT, if we can sample from q(xt−1|xt), then we can get the specific x0 from a random Gaussian noise. When the stepsize βt is very small, we have

q(xt|xt−1)=N(xt,1−βtxt−1,βtI)≈N(xt,xt−1,βtI),

let βtI=ϵ, we obtain

xt=xt−1+ϵ → xt−1=xt−ϵ,

which implies q(xt−1|xt)∼N(xt−1,xt,ϵ), a normal distribution. Especially when the number of the steps is large, we can have small enough βts to approach this approximation. However, to calculate q(xt−1|xt) needs the whole sequence. Here we choose a model pθ(x) to approximate q(x). We have

pθ(xt−1|xt)∼N(xt−1,μθ(xt,t),Σθ(xt,t)),pθ(x0:T)=pθ(xT)∏t=1Tpθ(xt−1|xt).
